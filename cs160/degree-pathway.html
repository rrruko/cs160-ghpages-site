OSU

Undergrad Program

OSU Corvallis offers a Computer Systems option and an Applied option for their computer science bachelor's degree program.

Here is the applied program.

According to this page, the applied option allows students to develop a custom program and submit it for approval.

OSU Cascades's undergrad CS program is geared toward web and mobile software development. Both Corvallis and Cascades require students to apply to the Engineering Professional Program to be eligible to register for higher-level courses, which involves passing a certain set of more general courses.

Grad Program

OSU Cascades campus does not offer a graduate program in CS, so graduate courses have to be taken at Corvallis campus.

The graduate CS program offers an M.S., M.Eng., and Ph.D.

PSU

Undergrad Program

Portland State's bachelor program is similar to OSU's. As far as I can tell, PSU doesn't have specialized options under the CS degree like OSU's Computer Systems and Applied programs. There is a sample 4-year course plan here. There is a capstone program, where students work in teams on software development projects for local companies. I think it's interesting that, judging by this graph of the prerequisites for each CS course, PSU expects students to already know a programming language going into the introductory course.

Grad Program

PSU offers a Master of Science and a Doctor of Philosophy. The M.S. gives students the option to write and defend a thesis, but they are also free not to. It's not clear to me what the incentive is to do the thesis, unless you just want the educational experience. It seems like you could just do that on your own if you really wanted to. It's mysterious.
